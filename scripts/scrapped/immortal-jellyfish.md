## The Twist
overlay: It grows younger.
visual: A soft glowing cluster of translucent polyps growing on a dark underwater rock, delicate tendrils reaching upward, faint blue and green bioluminescence, dreamy shallow depth of field, photorealistic, vertical composition.
narration: When it's starving, injured, or old, instead of dying it collapses into a blob and turns its adult cells back into a baby stage, growing into a fresh new polyp. It's like a butterfly turning back into a caterpillar. Then it grows up all over again, and it can repeat that cycle over and over.

## The Wrap

narration: In real life they still get eaten or catch disease, so they're not truly unkillable. But biologically, aging doesn't have to be a one-way street for them. Scientists study this tiny jellyfish hoping to understand our own cells. The most immortal thing alive is basically invisible.
