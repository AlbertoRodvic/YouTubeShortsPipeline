# The Blueberry That Isn't Blue
thumbnail: THE PINK BLUEBERRY

## Hook
visual: A single glowing wet vivid hot pink blueberry drops from above and bounces on a glossy black surface in a pitch dark black studio, water droplets splashing off on impact, dramatic rim light making the wet pink skin glow against the darkness, macro cinematic slow motion, deep black background
narration: This is not a raspberry. And no, it is not unripe. This is a one hundred percent real blueberry, and no matter how long you wait, it will never, ever turn blue.

## Introduction
visual: A garden bush covered in vivid hot pink blueberries, every single berry the same bright magenta-pink color with a small star-shaped crown, all the berries glowing the same intense pink among green leaves, warm afternoon light, professional nature photography, wide vertical composition
narration: Meet the pink blueberry. Same size, same shape, same little crown as every blueberry you've ever eaten. Except each berry ripens into a glowing shade of pink, as if someone turned nature's color settings the wrong way.

## Unique feature
overlay: IT NEVER TURNS BLUE
visual: An extreme macro close-up of a single glossy bright pink blueberry with a pale frosty bloom on a dark slate surface, studio lighting revealing the smooth waxy skin and star-shaped crown, shallow depth of field
narration: It skips blue entirely. The berries start yellow-green, blush slowly, and finish deep glossy pink. That's it. That is fully ripe. Even the flesh inside stays light pink and almost translucent.

## Origin
overlay: ORIGIN: USDA, 1991
visual: Rows of berry bushes heavy with small round vivid hot pink berries, every single berry the same bright magenta-pink color with a small star-shaped crown, in a sunlit American research orchard at golden hour, cinematic documentary style, long rows fading into the distance
narration: And it's not from a lab accident. A USDA berry breeder crossed two blueberries back in 1991, then spent fourteen years testing and perfecting the offspring. In 2005, the pink blueberry was finally released to the world.

## Experience
overlay: SWEETER THAN BLUE
visual: A white ceramic bowl filled to the top with small round berries in a vivid fuchsia magenta-pink color like pink bubblegum, every single berry the same bright pink shade with a small dry brown star-shaped crown, on a bright modern kitchen counter, soft window light, fresh and juicy look, food photography
narration: Now imagine popping one in your mouth. Up to twice as sweet as a regular blueberry, with a bright, citrusy tang and soft flowery notes. And that is exactly why they named it Pink Lemonade.

## Final wow
visual: A hero shot of one perfect glossy bright pink blueberry with a pale frosty bloom balanced on a mossy branch, glowing in a beam of soft sunlight against a dark forest background, magical cinematic mood
narration: Today it grows in ordinary backyards across America. It's a berry so pretty it even won an award as a decorative shrub, a fruit that looks photoshopped hanging on a real bush. So, would you eat a pink blueberry?
